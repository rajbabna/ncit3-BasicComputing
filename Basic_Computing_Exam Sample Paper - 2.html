Question 2: Spreadsheet Problem-Solving
Total Marks: 23
Scenario: You are asked to create a simple spreadsheet to track quarterly sales data for a small business. The business sells three products (A, B, and C) and needs to analyze sales performance across four quarters.
a) Planning the Spreadsheet:
i. Sketch or describe the structure of your spreadsheet with rows and columns (2 marks)
ii. List what data you would enter and what calculations you would include (3 marks)
b) Creating the Spreadsheet:
i. Enter sample data for sales of each product in each quarter (3 marks)
ii. Create formulas to calculate: total sales per product, total sales per quarter, and overall total (4 marks)
iii. Format the spreadsheet with appropriate column widths, alignment, and number formats (1 mark)
c) Using the Spreadsheet:
i. Create a graph showing sales trends across quarters (4 marks)
ii. Analyze which product had the highest sales and which quarter was most profitable (3 marks)
iii. Explain how changing a single sales value would affect your totals and graph (3 marks)
