Question 2: Spreadsheet - Sample Sales Data

Spreadsheet Planning Document

Purpose: Monthly sales tracking and analysis for retail store inventory management and business performance evaluation

Spreadsheet Specifications:
  • Track sales data across three product categories
  • Monitor performance over three-month period
  • Calculate totals, averages, and identify peak performance
  • Generate visual representation for management reports
  • Enable quick data modification and automatic recalculation

Monthly Sales Data (in USD)

Product Category January February March
Electronics $8,450.00 $9,200.00 $11,500.00
Clothing $5,320.00 $6,150.00 $7,800.00
Home & Garden $4,890.00 $5,670.00 $6,430.00

Formulas to Implement

Monthly Totals (SUM Function)

  • January Total: =SUM(B2:B4) → Result: $18,660.00
  • February Total: =SUM(C2:C4) → Result: $21,020.00
  • March Total: =SUM(D2:D4) → Result: $25,730.00

Category Averages (AVERAGE Function)

  • Electronics Average: =AVERAGE(B2:D2) → Result: $9,716.67
  • Clothing Average: =AVERAGE(B3:D3) → Result: $6,423.33
  • Home & Garden Average: =AVERAGE(B4:D4) → Result: $5,663.33

Maximum Sales Value (MAX Function)

  • Highest Sale: =MAX(B2:D4) → Result: $11,500.00 (Electronics - March)

Additional Calculations

  • Total Sales (All Categories, All Months): =SUM(B2:D4) → Result: $65,410.00
  • Quarter Average: =AVERAGE(B2:D4) → Result: $7,267.78
  • Percentage of Total:
    • Electronics: ($29,150 ÷ $65,410) × 100 = 44.58%
    • Clothing: ($19,270 ÷ $65,410) × 100 = 29.45%
    • Home & Garden: ($16,990 ÷ $65,410) × 100 = 25.97%

Slide 3: Password Security

Title: Creating & Maintaining Strong Passwords

Essential Elements:
  • Minimum 12 characters in length
  • Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Avoid personal information (birthdate, names, addresses)
  • Never share passwords with colleagues
  • Change passwords every 90 days
  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Avoid common passwords (123456, password, qwerty)

Red Flags to Avoid:

  • ❌ Writing passwords on sticky notes
  • ❌ Using "password" as your password
  • ❌ Sharing passwords in emails
  • ❌ Using the same password everywhere
  • ❌ Including usernames in passwords

Slide 4: Identifying Phishing Attacks

Title: Recognize & Avoid Phishing Threats

What is Phishing?
  • Fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information
  • Usually via deceptive emails, websites, or messages
  • Criminals impersonate trusted organizations
  • Goal: Steal passwords, credit card info, or personal data

Common Warning Signs:

1. Suspicious Sender Address

  • ❌ Example: noreply@paypa1.com (note the "1" instead of "l")
  • ✓ Always check full email address, not just display name

2. Urgent Language & Threats

  • "Verify your account immediately or it will be closed"
  • "Unusual activity detected - confirm now"
  • "Click here or you'll lose access"

3. Suspicious Links

  • Hover over links to see actual URL
  • ✓ Real bank links: https://www.yourbank.com
  • ❌ Phishing links: https://www.yourbank-secure.com.phishing.net

4. Generic Greetings

  • ❌ "Dear Customer" instead of your name
  • ✓ Legitimate companies usually personalize emails

5. Requests for Personal Information

  • Banks never ask for passwords via email
  • Social Security numbers requested via email = PHISHING
  • Credit card details in email = SCAM

6. Attachments from Unknown Senders

  • Executable files (.exe, .bat)
  • Suspicious Office documents
  • Always verify sender before opening

Real Examples to Watch Out For:

Suspicious Email What to Do
"Amazon Alert: Suspicious Activity Detected" Verify directly on Amazon.com (don't click the email link)
"Update Your Bank Account Now" Call your bank's official phone number
"Confirm Your PayPal Information" Go directly to PayPal.com in your browser

Slide 5: Data Protection Practices

Title: Safeguarding Company & Customer Data

1. Physical Security

  • Lock your computer when stepping away
  • Don't leave sensitive documents visible
  • Use privacy screens in public spaces
  • Secure office access with ID badges

2. Digital Security

  • Use VPN on public WiFi networks
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) where available
  • Encrypt sensitive files
  • Use approved storage solutions only

3. Data Handling

  • Only access data you need for your job
  • Don't share data with unauthorized people
  • Use secure methods for transferring files
  • Delete data when no longer needed

4. Incident Response

  • Report suspicious activity immediately
  • Don't attempt to investigate breaches yourself
  • Document what happened and when
  • Contact IT security team right away

Classification Levels:

Level Description Examples
Public Can be shared externally Marketing materials, press releases
Internal Restricted to employees Policies, procedures, team updates
Confidential Sensitive business data Financial records, strategies, plans
Restricted Highest security level Personal data, trade secrets, passwords

Slide 6: Safe Browsing Practices

Title: Protecting Yourself Online

1. Website Safety

  • Look for "https://" (secure connection)
  • Check for padlock icon in address bar
  • Verify website address is correct (not lookalikes)
  • Avoid clicking on ads or pop-ups

2. Download Safety

  • Download only from official sources
  • Verify file sizes (unusually small files = suspicious)
  • Scan downloads with antivirus before opening
  • Never download from untrusted sources

3. Social Media Awareness

  • Limit personal information shared publicly
  • Don't accept friend requests from strangers
  • Be cautious with location sharing
  • Review privacy settings regularly

4. Avoiding Malware

  • Keep software and OS updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Disable auto-play for USB drives
  • Be wary of "free" software downloads

5. Public WiFi Safety

  • Avoid banking/shopping on public WiFi
  • Use company VPN if available
  • Disable auto-connect features
  • Turn off file sharing on your device

Common Threats:

  • Ransomware: Locks your files, demands payment
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors your activity
  • Trojans: Appears legitimate but contains malware
  • Worms: Self-replicating programs that spread through networks

Additional Resources & Guidelines

Letter Writing Tips (Question 1)

  • Single space within paragraphs, double space between paragraphs
  • Use 1-inch margins on all sides
  • Standard font: Times New Roman, Arial, or Calibri (12pt)
  • Proper business letter format: sender info → date → recipient → salutation → body → closing
  • Keep language formal and professional
  • Proofread for spelling and grammar errors

Spreadsheet Formatting Tips (Question 2)

  • Header row: Bold, centered, light blue/gray background
  • Data rows: Left-aligned for text, right-aligned for numbers
  • Currency format: $#,##0.00
  • Column widths: Minimum 12-15 characters
  • Borders: Light gray, 0.5pt width
  • Freeze panes to keep headers visible while scrolling

Presentation Design Tips (Question 3)

  • Slide ratio: 16:9 widescreen format
  • Font sizes: Title (44-54pt), Body (28-32pt), Captions (18-24pt)
  • Color scheme: 2-3 main colors, avoid more than 5 colors total
  • Consistency: Same fonts, colors, and layout throughout
  • Contrast: Dark text on light background or vice versa
  • Images: High quality, relevant to content (min 300 DPI)
  • Text per slide: 5-6 lines maximum, use bullet points
  • Animation: Minimal use, focus on content clarity
